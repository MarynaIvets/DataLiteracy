A concise guide to reproducible research in health economics

Purpose and how to use this study guide

0.1 Objective

The objective of this study guide is to provide a resource to graduate students, early career PhD candidates and researchers performing applied empirical research in the field of management and social sciences analysing secondary data in health care markets. The majority of textbook examples and resources cover applications in the field of labour economics. Resources that explicitly cover reference to secondary data from health care providers and health insurances are scarce. Wile we will not cover any specific econometric method, the idea is to provide a resource that covers the major steps of a reproducible research project. We will introduce key resources in terms of text books and websites, focussing on freely avaialble material.

0.2 Learning objectives

The goal is to set up and carry out a data science project using secondary data. Students will learn all steps starting with hypothesis formulation, data generation and analysis, and presentation of empirical results.

At the end of this study guide, you will be able to:

  1. Recognize the features of using secondary health care data in empirical research.
  2. Execute the steps of are reproducible research project.
  3. Be able to implement an empirical research project.
  4. Recall the steps taken to execute a reproducible research project using secondary data.

0.3 Structure of the study guide

The study guide consists of five chapters that include the major steps of a reproducible research project. Each step of reproducible research is covered in three parts.

  1. An introduction to the basics concepts of each of these steps and key terminology.
  2. A resources box that provides important resources how to perform this step including main textbooks and reference to current web resources. We will emphasize open source materials.
  3. A showcase example of an empirical project reproduced based on the article: Hellerstein (1998)
